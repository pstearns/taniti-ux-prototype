A World of Natural Wonders

Spanning just under 500 square miles, Taniti packs an incredible variety of landscapes into a small area. You'll find everything from rugged rocky coastlines to lush, deep rainforests and a mountainous interior home to a safe, active volcano.

While the island was traditionally known for its fishing and agriculture, it has recently opened up to visitors. Most travelers start their journey in Taniti City, where you can admire native island architecture and relax on the white sands of Yellow Leaf Bay.
